### Additional Explorations

#### † Information Theory & Entropy (via Wordle)

- [Solving Wordle using information theory](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v68zYyaEmEA) — 3Blue1Brown uses Wordle as a central example to teach information theory and entropy, showing how to quantify the "information" gained from each guess and build an algorithm that plays optimally.
- [Oh, wait, actually the best Wordle opener is not "crane"…](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fRed0Xmc2Wg) — A follow-up from 3Blue1Brown correcting a minor bug, and diving deeper into how the optimal first guess is chosen via two-step exhaustive search over expected information.
- [Wordle Variants Collection](https://gist.github.com/maxspero/0a2f536b9561d829caf6bd994a34193d) — A community-maintained list of Wordle spin-offs including clones, multiplayer modes, multi-grid challenges, math variants (Nerdle, Primel), language/regional variants, and more.

#### ‡ Error Correction & Hamming Codes (via the Chessboard Puzzle)

- [The impossible chessboard puzzle](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wTJI_WuZSwE) — 3Blue1Brown presents a prisoner puzzle involving coins on a chessboard, connecting it to error-correcting codes and Hamming codes. Includes a proof that the puzzle is impossible when the number of squares is not a power of 2.
- [The almost impossible chessboard puzzle](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=as7Gkm7Y7h4) — Matt Parker (Stand-up Maths) and Grant Sanderson walk through the practical solution to the chessboard puzzle step by step, with an interactive demonstration.
